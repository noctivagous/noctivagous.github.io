nct-guiHardware-sideTrayKeys

On desktop computers, on keyboards
that have some thickness, there can be four
tray keys places on the sides of the keyboard
that activate the functions for sliding out and in
GUI trays from the sides of the screen,
for quick access to resources and information.

These keys can be configured with more than one purpose, such
as assigning the right tray to what is provided
by the current software application while the left
tray shows open applications.

The bottom one could be configured to show a centered
window with information and controls. 

Tapping the sides of an existing desktop keyboard
while envisioning trays sliding out and in while
give the impression of what this is about.
