We think then this has potential for an AI:

Yin and yang, Wuxing, and the trigrams
provide an immediate functional knowledge base 
that can be represented by direct language 
from the outset because of all of the
associations that already have a status
as being general.

Truths about the world are inherently present
in any knowledge graph of yin-yang and Wuxing.
For this reason, the following sentences could be easily
formed from a knowledgebase with all
of the concepts from ancient Chinese science:

- In the winter, it is colder, darker,
and more still.
- Midday is when there is the most
daylight.

These sentences would be generated from
the truths of yin and yang whereas in an
AI normally they would have to be manually
placed as rules or result from
statistical associations from training.
They demonstrate the potential of this
approach

Then when modern knowledge is added to the
truths and mapped to yin-yang and Wuxing
accurately, it can continue:

- "When a high pressure system (yang)
arrives, it disperses clouds and inclement
weather. High pressure systems feature sinking air
(yin). Low pressure systems, by contrast — with rising air
(yang), cloud formation, precipitation, instability, 
and "gathering" of weather are yin (receptive, condensing, moist)."
    - This is the kind of statement that the
    AI based on Wuxing and yin-yang may be
    able to generate by itself, just by
    having concepts provided.

Since in electromagnetism magnetic poles correspond
to north and south, with electrical
charges being positive and negative
and there is conceptual interdependence of
electricity and magnetism, discussions of
this topic will naturally be balanced.

Negative, both in math and electrical charges
would be mapped to yin.  Positive, both in
math and electrical charges would be mapped
to yang.  Then in discussions these would
be naturally integrated in the generated answers.

Wu-xing does map to the Periodic Table as well,
as the metals are predominantly made of Metal,
the noble gases can be said to be predominantly
made of Earth (stable, not needing anything), 
elements like Sulphur and Oxygen
map to being predominantly Fire, Nitrogen
predominantly made of Water.  They could
be placed in the knowledge graph as compositions.
